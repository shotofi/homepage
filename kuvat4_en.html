Kata pitures

Kata are sequences of movements grouped into applicable combinations. Kata works well as, among other things, a breathing and a focus exercise. Kata is usually done in a group and the goal is simultaneous execution starting from the breathing rhythm. The next 6 pictures are from a kata called Heian Shodan, which is taught starting from the beginner's course.


This movement can be used as a release from a grab and a follow up strike to the head of the attacker.

The final position after the kata ends. Focus and following the leader of the kata group should be maintained. The stance is called kokutsu dachi, where most of the body weight is on the back leg.

Distance

Distance is a key point and control of distance to your opponent or your training partner is an essential skill that develops as you gain training experience.


Yki has just completed a basic punch called oizuki but missed his opponent due to the defender distancing himself away from the punch.


Attacker focusing on his punch as the defender checks his distance.


If your distancing is correct your techniques will come easily. The defender on the left uses gedan barai to block the attackers punch, although the distance is not yet quite correct. Timing is also a key ingredient in a succesfull block and good timing and distancing can be used to affect the attackers balance.


Filip in the back checking his distance.


Often a only a small step is needed for correct distancing.


Examples of different people focusing on their techniques.


This and the next three pictures show more distance practice.

Close-in techniques

The opponent can sometimes get in close or he is deliberately given the chance to close in. In these cases you should apply close-in techniques such as wrestling, throws or locks.


A warm-up exercise for thighs.


Peter and Mika wrestling furiosly.


Yki throwing his opponent.


Throws and take-downs are often executed as counters for attacks. In these cases the techniques often try to redirect the attack and thus unbalance the attacker. Kisu being led to a take-down.


Practicing locks is shown in this and the next pic.


After a long session of practicing locks it is good to stretch and relieve your joints.
